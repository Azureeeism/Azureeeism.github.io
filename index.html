  • Bachelor Science in Library and Information Science

    This Program is the study of the development. deployment, and management of information resources in print, non-print, electronic and digital formats and services. Graduates of this program are being prepared to apply information technology to basic library operations and functions Moreover, they are trained to harness a range of bibliographical and online tools to support teaching, research, and other services. Graduates of this program are likewise expected to systematically organize, conserve, preserve, and restore information objects (e.g. realia museum pieces), historical and cultural documents (e.g. artifacts, archival documents, indigenous knowledge) and other intellectual properties (creative ideas and works by individuals and groups). The program was based on the Guidelines for Professional Library / Information Education Programs of the International Federation of the Library Associations and Institutions (IFLA) as well as on the curricula of the various library schools abroad

  • Diploma in Computer Technology

    The Diploma in Computer Technology (DCT) program is a comprehensive two year degree program designed to provide students with specialized skills in three distinct areas of the computing industry. The DCT program provides the approriate training on three (3) particular major: Networking, Application Development, and Multimedia. The Program allows students to choose a major based on their interests and career aspirations, enabling them to gain in depth knowledge and hands on experience in their chosen field
